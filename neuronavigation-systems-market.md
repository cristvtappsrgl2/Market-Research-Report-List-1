<p><ul><li>Optical System</li><li>Electromagnetic System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Neuronavigation systems are categorized into two main types: optical systems and electromagnetic systems. Optical systems utilize infrared cameras and reflective markers to track the position of surgical instruments in relation to preoperative images, providing high accuracy in neurosurgery. In contrast, electromagnetic systems employ magnetic fields to determine the location of instruments without the need for line-of-sight, allowing for more flexibility in complex anatomical areas. Both systems enhance surgical precision, minimize invasiveness, and improve patient outcomes in neurological procedures.</p></p>
