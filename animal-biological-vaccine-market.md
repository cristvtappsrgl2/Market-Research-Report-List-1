<p><p>The animal biological vaccine market includes various administration routes such as oral, subcutaneous, intramuscular, and others. Oral vaccines are delivered through feed or water, ensuring easy administration, especially in large animals. Subcutaneous vaccines are injected just beneath the skin, promoting localized immune response. Intramuscular vaccines are injected directly into muscle tissue, facilitating rapid absorption and effectiveness. The "others" category encompasses alternative methods, like intranasal or ocular routes, catering to specific species or health requirements. Each method has unique advantages based on the target animal and disease prevention needs.</p></p>
